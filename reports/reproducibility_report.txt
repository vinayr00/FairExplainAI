SECTION 1: PAPER REPRODUCTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. Dataset
- COMPAS Dataset: Both the paper and the implementation use the COMPAS recidivism dataset (compas-scores-two-years.csv).
- Target Variable: The target variable matches exactly (two_year_recid), representing whether the individual recidivated within two years.
- Protected Attributes: The protected attributes match exactly: race (African-American vs. Caucasian) and sex (Male vs. Female).
- Filtering & Preprocessing Assumptions: The paper does not explicitly mention the standard ProPublica cleaning rules (such as filtering days_b_screening_arrest within [-30, 30] or removing is_recid = -1). 
  * To ensure faithful reproduction, our implementation builds compas_baseline.csv without applying these ProPublica filters, maintaining the raw record structure as the paper did.
  * For the extension, compas_extended.csv applies these standard filters to guarantee scientific quality.

B. Data Preprocessing
- Feature Removal: The paper manually removes the following 4 features:
  * c_charge_desc
  * decile_score
  * r_charge_degree
  * r_charge_desc
  Our baseline feature selector reproduces this manually by restricting features to the 9 specified columns, effectively dropping all other features.
- Label Encoding: Our preprocessing uses standard encoding (mapping Female/Male and African-American/Caucasian as binary integers) matching the paper's description.
- Train/Test Split: Our train/test split matches the paper's 80/20 ratio.
- Target Variable Extraction: Matches exactly.

C. Machine Learning
- Algorithm: Both use Scikit-Learn's RandomForestClassifier.
- Number of Trees: n_estimators = 100 matches the paper's specification exactly.
- Random State: Seeded appropriately (RANDOM_STATE = 42) for deterministic reproduction.
- Split: 80/20 train/test split implemented.

D. Counterfactual Explanations
- Framework: The implementation utilizes the Python dice_ml package to generate counterfactual examples.
- Protected Attributes: Counterfactuals are generated by perturbing the protected attributes race and sex while holding continuous features constant.
- Query Cases: Counterfactual instances target the two specific profiles documented in Section III.I of the paper:
  1. Race: Caucasian; Sex: Female
  2. Race: African-American; Sex: Male
- Methodology: Matches the paper's recourse verification setup.

E. Fairness
- Framework: Both utilize the Fairlearn toolkit.
- Metric: Demographic Parity difference is used.
- Mitigation Algorithm: Fairlearn's post-processing ThresholdOptimizer enforces demographic parity.
- Retraining & Evaluation: Evaluated using selection rates across demographic slices, matching the paper.

F. Results
- Reported vs. Observed Metrics:
  * Paper Baseline Accuracy: 79.26%
  * Paper Mitigated Accuracy: 60.13%
  * Our Baseline Accuracy: 63.55%
  * Our Mitigated Accuracy (Extended): 67.13%

- Diagnostic Explanation of Mismatch:
  Our empirical tests on the COMPAS raw dataset show:
  1. If we train a Random Forest classifier by dropping only the 4 columns named in the paper, we get a test accuracy of 97.16% due to target leakage (because columns like is_recid, v_decile_score, score_text are left in).
  2. If we train the model strictly on the 9 features listed in the paper's Section III.A, the actual maximum accuracy is 62.92% - 63.55%.
  3. The paper's reported baseline accuracy of 79.26% indicates that the authors either:
     * Suffered from target leakage due to other unmentioned columns left in their dataset (e.g. score_text), OR
     * Encountered train/test data leakage (e.g., duplicated records or non-stratified leakage).
     * When they applied Demographic Parity post-processing to their leaky model, accuracy dropped to 60.13%.

G. Figures
- Prediction Graphs / Selection Rates: Generated equivalent plots of accuracy and selection rates by demographic group.
- Counterfactual Dot-Plots: Implemented dot plots mapping prediction flips across instances, corresponding to Figures 3, 4, 5, and 6 in the paper.
